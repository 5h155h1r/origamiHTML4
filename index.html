Origami Fox

Fun Facts About Foxes

  • A group of foxes is called a skulk or leash.
  • Foxes have whiskers on their legs and face, which help them to navigate.
  • Grey foxes can retract their claws like cats do.
  • A male is called a 'dog fox' while a female is called a 'vixen'

Origami Camel

Fun Facts About Camels

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Origami Chameleon

Fun Facts About Chameleons

  • Chameleons come in a lot of different shapes and sizes
  • Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues
  • Chameleons are tree-huggers
  • Chameleons take a while to hatch, and don’t live very long

Origami Pigeon

Fun Facts About Pigeons

  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.
  • Pigeons and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals.

Origami Panda

Fun Facts About Panda

  • They have great camouflage for their environment
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears
  • They spend a lot of their day eating.
  • Bamboo is critical to their diet
